# Pasta Cooking Reference

## General Pasta Cooking Guidelines

### Water and Salting

- **Salt the water generously** — aim for a concentration that tastes like seawater
- Use enough water that pasta can move freely while cooking
- Salt should be added before bringing water to a boil

### Cooking Time and Doneness

- **Follow package instructions** if using Italian-brand pasta — this typically indicates *al dente* (firm to the bite with a slight resistance in the center)
- Al dente pasta has better texture when combined with sauce and is less likely to become mushy
- Test for doneness at the lower end of the suggested range if you prefer firmer pasta

### Transferring Pasta and Sauce

- **Use tongs** for long pasta like spaghetti or linguine
- **Use a spider strainer** (large scoop with holes) for shorter shapes like penne or rigatoni
- **Do not drain the pasta** — use tongs or strainer to transfer pasta directly from the boiling water to the sauce
- Keep the pasta water in the pot for ladling as needed

### Combining Pasta and Sauce

- Transfer hot pasta directly from the boiling water into the sauce using the spider strainer or tongs
- Ladle pasta water into the sauce as needed — start with a full ladle (~6 oz) per serving, then adjust
- The starchy pasta water helps:
  - Adjust sauce consistency
  - Emulsify fat-based sauces
  - Coat pasta evenly with a silky finish
- It's a back-and-forth adjustment: add water if the sauce is too thick, let it reduce slightly if too thin
- Toss gently but constantly to combine and coat evenly

## Serving Techniques

### The Twirl Method (For Long Pasta)

1. Use a 6 oz ladle or large shallow spoon and tongs together
2. Gather a portion of pasta with the tongs, twirling slightly to form a nest
3. Hold the ladle underneath to catch sauce
4. Lift the twirled portion onto the plate
5. Pour the sauce in the ladle over the top

This creates an elegant presentation with even sauce distribution and is common in Italian restaurants.

### The Simple Spoon Method (For Any Pasta)

1. Use a pasta fork or large spoon
2. Gather a portion directly into a bowl or plate
3. Ladle sauce over the top
4. Serve immediately while hot

---

## Tips for Success

- Keep pasta water warm and accessible — it's your secret ingredient
- Pasta continues to cook slightly after draining, so stop cooking a minute or two before you think it's done
- Starchy pasta water is better than plain water for adjusting sauce consistency
- Never rinse cooked pasta — the starch on the surface helps sauce adhere
- Serve immediately in warmed bowls or plates
